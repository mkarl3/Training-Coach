01  Logo & WordmarkA

The wordmark is set in Press Start 2P with a hard red drop-shadow and a faint screen-glow. It is the only place the pixel typeface appears at large size. Treat it as an arcade title card.

WATT SMITH

Clearspace. Keep a margin of one capital "W" (in the pixel face) on all sides. Nothing intrudes.

Minimum size. Never below 16px cap-height on screen — under that the pixels mush and it stops reading.

Drop-shadow. Red #E84444 offset 3px down/right is mandatory. The glow is optional and only on dark.

Lockup. Coach Wattson may sit to the left of the wordmark at equal cap-height for app icons and splash screens.

✕ Don't recolor the wordmark or drop the red shadow.
✕ Don't set body text or numbers in the pixel face.
✕ Don't stretch, skew, or add bevels/gradients.
02  Coach WattsonB

Coach Wattson is the app's voice — the face on the early-warning dashboard and the coach interface. An old pro: blunt when the numbers demand it, warm when you've earned it. He carries the personality so the data screens stay clinical. Red-and-white casquette, blue track jacket, stopwatch on a cord. He is live SVG, never a flat image — recolorable, sharp at any size, and all moods derive from one base.

05  The Core PrincipleE

The single rule the whole brand hangs on: nostalgia lives in the art, never in the data. The retro feeling comes from texture and character; the metrics stay clinical and legible.

✓ 8-bit / CRT carries

  • Coach Wattson sprite
  • NES palette & scanlines
  • Notched pixel-border windows
  • Dialogue boxes, hearts, stars
  • The logo & arcade accents

✕ Stays clean & legible

  • Numbers & metrics (Plex Mono)
  • Labels & body (Outfit)
  • Charts — smooth SVG lines
  • Tables & data grids
  • Anything you read to decide
06  CRT & TextureF

The CRT layer is what makes this feel like a cartridge and not a web app. Three ingredients — used on chrome, dialed back on data.

✓ Apply to

  • Scanlines — global overlay, ~13% opacity, 3px pitch
  • Vignette — soft darkened screen edges
  • Pixel-notched borders + hard offset shadows on every window
  • Subtle glow on the logo (dark backgrounds only)

✕ Hold back on

  • Never let scanlines drop metric contrast — data panels keep solid fills
  • No flicker/wobble on screens you read numbers from
  • No curvature distortion over charts or tables
  • Vignette stays off dense data views

This very document uses the full treatment — scanlines, vignette, glow — so you can judge the maximum. In-app, the dashboard runs it lighter.

09  Voice & ToneI

Wattson states the number first, then what to do. Encouraging when earned, blunt when needed — never hype, never insulting. App UI copy stays plain and factual; Wattson adds the warmth.
